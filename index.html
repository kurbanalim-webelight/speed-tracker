Step by Step

How It Works

Think of it like a toll booth speed camera — but for cricket. Here's what happens every time someone bowls.

🏏

Step 1 — Bowler Releases the Ball

The ball leaves the bowler's hand. The radar is waiting behind the wicket, quietly scanning the air in front of it.

📡

Step 2 — Radar Detects the Ball

Invisible radio waves bounce off the flying ball. The frequency change tells the radar exactly how fast it's going. This is the Doppler Effect — same as a traffic speed gun.

🧠

Step 3 — Brain Filters the Data

The ESP32 microcontroller filters out bat swings and player movement, then picks the highest valid speed from the delivery.

📶

Step 4 — Sent via Bluetooth

Speed is sent wirelessly via Bluetooth (BLE) — same as your earphones. No internet, no Wi-Fi, no data plan needed.

📱

Step 5 — Speed on Screen

The phone app receives the number and displays it in large bold text. Ball to screen: under 1 second. Saved to history automatically.

How the Radar Works

Same tech as a speed gun.

The radar sends out radio waves — invisible to the eye. When a cricket ball flies through, the waves hit it and bounce back. Because the ball is moving, the returning wave has a slightly different frequency. That difference tells us the speed. Scientists call this the Doppler Effect.

Imagine shouting "hello" at a moving wall. The echo comes back at a different pitch. That pitch difference is how radar measures speed — no camera, no contact, no setup.

Reliability

The System Handles It.

Every possible problem is handled automatically. Here's how.

🏏
Bat Swing Triggers System

Problem: Bat swings at ~35 km/h get detected.

Fix: Minimum threshold raised to 40 km/h after calibration. Bat swings are filtered out.

📶
Bluetooth Disconnects

Problem: Phone goes out of range mid-match.

Fix: ESP32 auto-re-advertises. Flutter reconnects automatically. Radar keeps running. No reset needed.

🔋
Power Bank Runs Out

Problem: Battery dies during a match.

Fix: Recharge and plug back in — restarts in seconds. 20,000mAh gives 12–20 hours, so this rarely happens.

🚶
Players Walking Near Radar

Problem: A fielder walks near the sensor.

Fix: Walking is 5–10 km/h. System ignores everything below 20 km/h automatically.
